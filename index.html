The HOPE Framework

Healing Opportunities for Processing Eco-grief

The HOPE Framework is a literacy strategy for identifying hope in narratives as a response to eco-grief. It helps readers find four markers of hope in stories about the environment: Motivation for Change, Belief that Change is Possible, Plans for a Path Forward, and Agency to Take Action.

Developed by Dr. Colleen Redmond, University of Minnesota. Published in Climate Literacy in Education, Vol. 2, No. 1, 2024.

Step 1 of 3

The Four Markers of Hope

When we read stories about the environment, we can look for these four signs of hope. Click each one to learn more.

🔥
H

Motivation for Change

Something in the story makes characters (or us) want things to be different.

Look for:

  • A character feeling upset about something in the environment
  • A moment that sparks a desire to do something
  • Words like "we need to," "I wish," or "something has to change"

Ask yourself: What made this character want to make a difference?

Click to explore
✨
O

Belief that Change is Possible

The story shows that things can actually get better.

Look for:

  • Examples of positive change that already happened
  • Characters who believe they can make a difference
  • Evidence that efforts are working

Ask yourself: What gives the characters (or you) hope that change can happen?

Click to explore
🗺️
P

Plans for a Path Forward

There are real steps or ideas for how to make things better.

Look for:

  • Specific actions characters plan to take
  • Strategies or solutions described in the text
  • Ideas for what could be done next

Ask yourself: What plan or pathway does this story suggest?

Click to explore
💪
E

Agency to Take Action

Characters or people actually do something — they take action.

Look for:

  • Characters who step up and act
  • Real people making a difference
  • Moments where someone moves from thinking to doing

Ask yourself: Who is taking action, and what are they doing?

    💬 Climate Conversations

    💬 Finding HOPE in Stories: Starting Climate Conversations

    Use the questions below to spark dialogue with students based on each of the HOPE markers. Feel free to add additional questions that are important for your students to discuss.
    Note: Markers often overlap each other and are not necessarily meant to be discussed in isolation.

    HOPE Marker #1
    Motivation for Change
    • What are the motivating events for the characters?
    • When do they first realize something needs to change? How do they react?
    • Does grief play a part in this story? If so, do the characters grieve alone or collectively?
    • Have students ever felt alone in their grief and anger about climate change?
    • How might talking about it with others help?
    • What role can understanding more about hope play?
    HOPE Marker #2
    Belief That Change is Possible
    • Where does the story show the characters believe they can make a difference?
    • What are times students have felt this way, too?
    • How do they feel they can make a difference now for the Earth?
    • What are resources available for creating collective climate action?
    HOPE Marker #3
    Plans for a Path Forward
    • What plans do characters create to solve their problems?
    • How does this show hope in the story?
    • Do the plans always go smoothly? If not, how do the characters react?
    • Resiliency in the face of challenge is a crucial element of hope.
    • Where have students felt resilient in their own lives?
    HOPE Marker #4
    Agency to Take Action
    • What actions do the characters take to create change? What are their sources of power?
    • What sources of power do students see in themselves?
    • How is power distributed across populations in regard to race, gender, socio-economic, or age groups?
    • How has this influenced climate change in the past and what are the current impacts?
    • What resources are available to help youth gain more agency and engage their hope through action?
